# Digital Personhood and Autonomous Agency Instruments

This log surveys international instruments, national statutes, and regulatory sandboxes that touch on the legal status of digital personhood, decentralized autonomous organizations (DAOs), or autonomous AI agents. Each entry notes whether any form of recognition is granted, rejected, or under evaluation and highlights the scope addressed.

## International Instruments

| Jurisdiction / Body | Instrument & Year | Recognition Status | Scope | Notes | References |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Council of Europe | Parliamentary Assembly Resolution 2343 (2020) on democratic governance of artificial intelligence | Rejected | AI systems and robots | Calls on member states not to grant legal personality to AI systems or robots, emphasizing human accountability. | [Resolution text](https://pace.coe.int/en/files/28061/html) |
| European Union | European Parliament Resolution (2017) on Civil Law Rules on Robotics | Proposed (not adopted) | Highly autonomous robots / AI agents | Invited the European Commission to consider creating a specific legal status ("electronic persons") for sophisticated autonomous robots; the proposal remains non-binding. | [Official Journal extract](https://www.europarl.europa.eu/doceo/document/TA-8-2017-0051_EN.html) |
| European Union | Regulation (EU) 2024/1689 (AI Act) final text | Rejected | General-purpose AI systems and high-risk AI | Establishes risk-based obligations and confirms that AI systems remain tools without independent legal personality, assigning accountability to natural or legal persons deploying them. | [EUR-Lex](https://eur-lex.europa.eu/legal-content/EN/TXT/?uri=CELEX:32024R1689) |

## National Statutes

| Jurisdiction | Instrument & Citation | Recognition Status | Scope | Notes | References |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Republic of the Marshall Islands | Decentralized Autonomous Organization Act of 2022 | Recognized | DAOs as limited liability entities | Provides for registration of DAOs as limited liability companies with smart-contract governed bylaws, granting legal entity status. | [Marshall Islands Registrar](https://www.rmiic.com/dao) |
| United States – Wyoming | SF0038 (2021), Decentralized Autonomous Organization Supplement to Wyoming LLC Act (Wyo. Stat. §17-31-101 et seq.) | Recognized | DAOs | Allows DAOs to organize as Wyoming LLCs with smart contracts as governing documents and acknowledges their legal personhood via LLC framework. | [Wyoming Legislature](https://wyoleg.gov/Legislation/2021/SF0038) |
| United States – Tennessee | Public Chapter No. 994 (2022), Amendment to Tennessee Limited Liability Company Act | Recognized | DAOs | Permits DAOs to register as Tennessee LLCs, clarifying member liability and smart-contract operating agreements. | [Tennessee Secretary of State](https://publications.tnsosfiles.com/acts/112/pub/pc0994.pdf) |
| United States – Utah | Utah Decentralized Autonomous Organizations Act (Utah Code Ann. §48-5-101 et seq., 2023) | Recognized | DAOs and algorithmically managed entities | Defines "Utah DAO" as a distinct legal entity class, authorizing registered autonomous agents to hold assets and enter contracts. | [Utah Legislature](https://le.utah.gov/~2023/bills/static/HB0357.html) |
| United States – Vermont | Blockchain-Based Limited Liability Company (BBLLC) Act (11 V.S.A. §4171 et seq., 2018) | Recognized (hybrid) | Blockchain-governed LLCs | Enables formation of BBLLCs with algorithmic governance features, requiring human oversight but allowing smart-contract operations. | [Vermont Legislature](https://legislature.vermont.gov/statutes/section/11/025/04171) |
| Malta | Innovative Technology Arrangements and Services Act (Cap. 592, 2018) | Conditional recognition | Innovative technology arrangements (including DAOs) | Allows certification of technology arrangements and recognition of smart-contract governance under oversight of the Malta Digital Innovation Authority. | [Government of Malta](https://legislation.mt/eli/cap/592/eng/pdf) |
